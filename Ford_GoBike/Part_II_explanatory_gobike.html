# get when (in terms of time of the day) bikes are popular
plt.figure(figsize=[14, 6])
sns.histplot(data=df, x='start_hour', binwidth=1, kde=True);
plt.xlabel('Start time [hour]')
plt.ylabel('Count')
plt.title('Bike usage distribution: during the day')
plt.xticks([t for t in range(24) if t%2==0]);

Finding: The distribution of bike usage throughout the day is bimodal, indicating the presence of two distinct peaks. The first peak occurs at 8 am, suggesting a significant surge in bike usage during the early morning hours, possibly coinciding with the start of the workday. The second peak is observed at 5 pm, signifying another notable increase in bike usage in the late afternoon, which could correspond to the end of the workday.

fig, axes = plt.subplots(2)
fig.set_size_inches(12, 8)
fig.suptitle('Bike usage: during the weekdays')
ax1 = sns.countplot(ax=axes[0], data=df, x='start_weekday', order=day_order);
ax2 = sns.barplot(ax=axes[1], data=df, x='start_weekday', y='duration_sec', order=day_order);

axes[0].set(ylabel='Trip count', xlabel='',)
axes[1].set(ylabel='Average trip duration [sec]', xlabel='')

for i in range(5, 7):
    ax1.patches[i].set_facecolor('tab:green')
    ax2.patches[i].set_facecolor('tab:green')

Finding:

Weekday vs. Weekend Trip Count: The analysis reveals a noticeable decrease in the overall trip count during weekends compared to weekdays. This suggests that there is a discernible difference in bike usage patterns between weekdays and weekends.

Average Trip Duration: Interestingly, despite the lower trip count on weekends, the average trip duration shows an increase during this period. This indicates that while fewer trips are taken on weekends, the individual bike rides tend to be longer in duration.

We should consider the possibility that, weekdays may be characterized by more utilitarian bike usage, such as commuting to work or running errands, whereas weekends may see a greater emphasis on leisure and recreational activities.
